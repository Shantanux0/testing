How It Works

1. The "Perfect Swap" Engine

The core of SkillSwap is our Weighted Skill Matching Algorithm. It doesn't just look for keywords; it calculates a compatibility score (0.0 to 1.0) based on:

  • Test Scores (30%): Verified proficiency via our Test Portal.
  • Proficiency Gap (20%): Matches peers or mentors (1 level above).
  • Availability (10%): Real-time intersection of schedules.
  • Goal Alignment (10%): Matching learning goals with teaching motivations.

2. The Test Portal (Verification)

To maintain quality, every teacher must pass a rigorous test. If they score below 10/15, they cannot list themselves as a teacher for that specific skill. This ensures that the "expertise" on our platform is real.

3. Real-Time Collaboration

Once a match is made, users can coordinate via our integrated messaging system and join a Teaching Room featuring code playgrounds and meeting agendas.

Artificial Intelligence Part

SkillSwap isn't just a wrapper; it uses AI strategically across three main areas:

  • Dynamic Question Generation: When a user starts a skill test, our AIQuestionGeneratorService creates 15 unique, hard-level questions on the fly. This prevents cheating and keeps the testing fresh.
  • Sentiment Analysis: We analyze feedback text to detect the true quality of a session, influencing the teacher's Reputation Score beyond just star ratings.
  • Recommendation Engine: A behavioral "learn" loop that tracks which types of mentors a user interacts with most, refining future suggestions.

Counter Questions & Answers

Q1: Does your project have an LLM model integrated?

Yes, we use an interface-driven AI architecture. Currently, it's integrated with Google Gemini and OpenAI. We chose an interface-driven approach so we can swap models without changing the core business logic (Model Agnosticism).

Q2: How do you handle the swapping of models?

We use the Strategy Pattern in the backend. We have an AIQuestionGeneratorService interface. We can provide different implementations (GeminiImpl, OpenAIImpl) and switch them via configuration settings or load-balancing.

Q3: How do you train the data for your AI?

For the MVP, we leverage Prompt Engineering on top of pre-trained LLMs. We provide the AI with a structured "System Prompt" that contains our specific constraints (e.g., JSON output format, difficulty levels, skill context). The "learning" part happens in our Recommendation Engine, where we store user interaction data to personalize future matches.

Q4: What happens if the AI service is down?

We have a Static Question Bank fallback. If the AI fails to generate questions, the system automatically pulls high-quality, pre-verified questions from our database to ensure zero downtime for the user.

Q5: Is the matching algorithm real-time?

Yes. When a user searches for a swap, the backend executes the weighted matching logic against all active profiles in the database, considering their latest test scores and availability schedules stored in JSON format.
